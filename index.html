Makgabeng Heritage Research & Publishing

MAK HERP cc (Makgabeng Heritage Research & Publishing cc) is a 100% black-owned company whose core focus is to identify, develop, promote, protect, manage, conserve and preserve HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE, particularly those of the communities that had been marginalised and suppressed previously. The histories and stories of the majority of communities in South Africa (blacks) have not been fully documented and represented in the mainstream, and MAK HERP cc thus aims to contribute in having those histories and stories brought out of the periphery in order for them to occupy their right place, which is the centre stage. MAK HERP cc aims to use various forms, particularly literature, dance and music to research, analyse and document such histories and stories. The organisation is led by two professionals, Dr. Tlou Setumu and L.P. Thoka. Dr. Tlou Setumu is an academic, historian, researcher, author and heritage practitioner, while L.P. Thoka is a teacher, cultural practitioner, author and poet. Their combined knowledge, expertise and skill is used to dig deep into the past in order to document the HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE of ordinary South Africans, especially in the poor rural communities whose voices had never been given any chance previously. The team aims to work and partner with other professionals and stakeholders when a need arises for specific projects and programmes. MAK HERP cc’s main focus areas are on community awareness about the richness of their HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE; research; and documentation into books, documentaries on CDs and DVDs. Literature, dance and music are important vehicles with which communities will be assisted to express their art forms. For these activities of awareness, research, documentation and distribution, funding is required. Details of how this funding will be utilised will be dealt with in the subsequent paragraphs. Preserving our HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE by documenting them and expressing them as art forms have important benefits which include, but not limited to the following:
 Will express and showcase their art forms such as in literature, dance and music.
 Communities will have knowledge of their HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE which will enhance their pride and spiritualities.
 Documented HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE of communities will be used for educational purposes.
 Books, music, documentaries CDs and DVDs with content of researched HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE can be sold to benefit communities economically.

These are the objectives of MAK HERP cc

    Make communities aware of the abundant heritage resources among which are potentially beneficial that can be tapped into.
    Research and document rich HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE of communities.
    Use the researched content of communities’ HISTORY, HERITAGE and CULTURE into literature, dance and music.
    Conserve and preserve stories and by publishing them into books.
    Use technological and digital devices to conserve and preserve the researched works into dance and music documentary forms on CDs and DVDs.
    Preserve the rich dance and music – especially indigenous genres – by recording them into distributable albums.
    Marketing distribution of the final products of books, CDs and DVDs for public consumption.

Books by Makgabeng Heritage and Publishing

The Life and Struggle of a Commander:
Biography of Bra Ike Maphoto
Mak Herp
2012
VIEW

This book is about an overview of how indigenous black African communities lost their land to the Boer and British colonial forces – but were later expected to buy that land which they did not sell in the first place. This book uses the Makgabeng area – a rural area of over thirty villages around the Makgabeng Mountain, in the north-western corner of Limpopo Province, South Africa – as a case study. It picks up the land issue from the earliest occupation of land in the Makgabeng area by the Khoisan and Bantu-speaking communities through to the mid-19th century when land occupation and ownership were affected by the arrival of Europeans. It continues to examine communal identity creation in that area until about 2012.

The Life and Struggle of a Commander

The book is about the life and struggle of Isaac Lesibe Maphoto, who is fondly known as Bra Ike. He is an African National Congress (ANC)/ Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK) veteran who earned his stripes by joining the struggle against apartheid oppression when he left rural villages and looked for work in what is today Gauteng Province of South Africa towards the end of the 1940s. His political activities soon attracted the apartheid security forces. He skipped the country in the early 1960s and went on to be trained militarily in the Soviet Union. He was arrested in 1968 during the Sipolilo guerrilla campaign in Southern Rhodesia where the ANC’s MK forces in partnership with ZAPU’s ZIPRA forces were fighting bush war against the Rhodesian white minority regime of Ian Smith. He was then sentenced to death. His sentence was later commuted to life, and he was released in 1980 with the independence of Southern Rhodesia which became Zimbabwe. Bra Ike continued the struggle until apartheid was knocked out at negotiations table and after 1994, he served in a democratic dispensation in various capacities.
