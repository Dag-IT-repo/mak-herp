Biography          

Biography of Tlou Setumu

Born in the rural Makgabeng area (Limpopo’s Blouberg municipal area) in a poor family, raised by a single, sickly, unemployed mother. Completed matric in 1983. Worked mainly as private teacher – but enrolled for BA which was obtained in 1993. Specialised in History in all studies. Enrolled for one-year Higher Education Diploma in 1994 at the University of the North (now University of Limpopo). Completed BA Honours degree with UNISA, part time in 1998. Continued teaching until enrolling for MA degree in 1999 with University of Pretoria, and worked part time there as Museum Assistant at the Van Tilburg Collection, and as assistant Tutor in the UP Department of History.

Joined the South African Heritage Resources Agency (SAHRA) in Limpopo Province as a researcher and project manager in 2001. Registered PhD in History with RAU (now University of Johannesburg) in 2002. Employed by Limpopo Department of Sport, Arts and Culture in 2004 to establish Limpopo provincial heritage authority, LIHRA. Completed PhD programme in May 2010. Published over 15 books which are mostly researched works on History, Heritage and Culture

Married to Reginah and are blessed with three daughters, Phuti (17), Maphuti(15) and Manoko (President) (9). Currently (2015) works as Head of Department in the Public Heritage Education Department at the Robben Island Museum.
